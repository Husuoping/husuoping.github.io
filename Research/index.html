A Four-Order N-Path Switch-Cap Filter in 65nm (expected to tape out in Oct. 2015)

  • Design a Two-Order Low Pass Filter(LPF) and transfer to Band Pass Filter（BPF）
  • Design 4 No-Overlap Clocks by Utilization of modulo-4 counter

In this project, we aim to design a N-Path Filter, which is used as channel selection at the input of RF receivers and withstands 0dBm blocker. Because of the effect of the parasite capacitor of both the amplifiers and the capacitor arrays, the pass band is not as smooth as we expected, most of our work is to cover this effect.

A Dual-Band Frequency Tunable Complex Filter with stable Quality-Factor in different temperatures

  • Analyzed and solved the temperature deviation of complex filter
  • Taped out and tested the chip with expected result
  • Measured gain deviation at the edge of the bandwidth is less than 0.4dB which
    means the Q is quite stable (-25°C to 100°C)

An active-RC 5th Chebyshev complex filter with stable quality factor (Q) in different temperatures for global navigation satellite system (GNSS) receivers is proposed in this research. Considering GNSS application where the complex filter needs to work in extreme temperature, this research focused on Q-tuning method especially for temperature compensation. We used combination current as bias of amplifier so that we are able to compensate gain deviation as temperature. Finally, a complex filter is presented and it achieves the goal that within temperature varying from -25 °C to 100 °C, the Q of this filter is quite stable.
