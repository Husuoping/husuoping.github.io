ACHIEVEMENTS

Accepted paper

  • Suoping Hu, Dongpo Chen, Jin Jin, Tingting Mo. A Dual-Band Frequency Tunable Complex Filter with Stable Quality-Factor in Different Temperatures, which has been accepted by the IEEE ASICON.

To perform channel selection and image rejection in low-IF receivers, complex filter is commonly employed. However, in order to overcome the process variation and the nonideality of the integrator, frequency-tuning and Q-tuning circuits are indispensable in the complex filter. Considering GNSS application where the complex filter needs to work in extreme temperature, this paper focused on Q-tuning method especially for temperature compensation. First, a Q-tuning scheme relaxing the requirement of integrator is presented. Then the temperature characteristic of the integrator GBW is analyzed and bias current adjustment method is proposed to cancel the GBW deviation due to temperature. At last, a 5th Chebyshev complex filter is designed as example to illustrate the effect of the proposed Q-tuning method.
In this paper, a complex filter with stable Q in different temperatures is presented and it achieves the goal that within temperature varying from -25 °C to 100 °C, the Q of this filter is quite stable. This filter is also able to work at 4MHz with 2 or 4MHz bandwidth as well as at 16MHz with 10MHz bandwidth.
